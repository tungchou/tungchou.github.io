Tung Chou

I am currently a specially appointed assistant professor (特任助教) at Miyaji's lab (宮地研究室) at Osaka University (大阪大学), Japan.

Before I was a postdoctoral researcher (Sep. 2016--Jan. 2017) at the Cryptographic Implementations group at Technische Universiteit Eindhoven, the Netherlands.
Before I was a PhD student (Sep. 2012--Aug. 2016) at the Cryptographic Implementations group at Technische Universiteit Eindhoven, the Netherlands.
My advisors were Daniel J. Bernstein and Tanja Lange.

Before I was a research assistant at Institute of Information Science (中央研究院), Academia Sinica, Taiwan, under supervision of 楊柏因 (Bo-Yin Yang).
Before I was a master student at the Department of Electrical Engineering of National Taiwan University (台灣大學), Taiwan, under supervision of 鄭振牟 (Chen-Mou Cheng).

Research projects

Code-based: QcBits Constant-time small-key code-based cryptography.
McBits Fast constant-time code-based cryptography.
Elliptic-curve: Sandy2x A Curve25519 implementation tailored for the Sandy Bridge/Ivy Bridge microarchitectures.
fastOT An optmized OT protocol.
BADA55 (pronounced "bad-ass"): Manipulating curve standards.
Multivariate: mqchallenge Breaking MQ challenges.
libFES Fast Exhaustive Search for Polynomial Systems over F2.
Symmetric: Auth256 A MAC designed for low bit operation count.
